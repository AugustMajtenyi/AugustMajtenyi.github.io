Lifecycle Investing

Objective: Investigate different ways of implementing Lifecycle Investing

Context: For an explanation of Lifecycle Investing I recommend reading the book or my attempt of an overview.


From Theory to Practice

There is a bit of a problem when it comes to actually implementing this strategy. Taxes! It is briefly mentioned in the book that Roth accounts are preferred for this reason, but it does not discuss the implications of this for people that have money to save beyond what their retirement account allows. The way I see it, there are two options for dealing with this:

1: Overcompensate your retirement account LEAPs with implied leverage above 2:1.

2: Use a responsible amount of margin

Exploring the Value of LEAPs

This Options Calculator allows people to easily find the implied leverage, implied interest, and value relative to the Black Scholes formula.



I have found that as leverage increases beyond 2:1, implied annual interest is inconsistent and sometimes exceeds expected growth


Additionally, this strategy requires that the stock price NEVER defaults belaw the strike price. Increase leverage beyond 3:1 greatly increases your odds of this happening. At the very least, this strategy requires greater attention and decreased profits from increased turnover loss.
