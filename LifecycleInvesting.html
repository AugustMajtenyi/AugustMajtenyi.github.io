Lifecycle Investing

Objective: Investigate different ways of implementing Lifecycle Investing

Context: For an explanation of Lifecycle Investing I recommend reading the book or my attempt of an overview.


From Theory to Practice

There is a bit of a problem when it comes to actually implementing this strategy. Taxes! It is briefly mentioned in the book that Roth accounts are preferred for this reason, but it does not discuss the implications of this for people that have money to save beyond what their retirement account allows. The way I see it, there are two options for dealing with this:


1: Overcompensate your retirement account LEAPs with leverage greater than 2:1

2: Use a responsible amount of margin

Exploring the Value of LEAPs

This Options Calculator allows people to easily find the implied leverage, implied interest, and value relative to the Black Scholes formula.




I have found that as leverage increases beyond 2:1, implied annual interest is inconsistent and sometimes exceeds expected growth. Moreso, more leverage increases your odds of the stock price defaulting below the strike price.



Safe Amount of Margin?


This Margin Calculator allows people to easily find their current leverage and determine what changes should be made to rebalance to the desired leverage.


This strategy requires that the account not lose money from a margin call. To test this, the document also helps you to compare investments in different market conditions.


I have even gone as far as testing various margin strategies based on past market data. The file is real big, but I'm happy to talk it through with you if you are interested.




Conclusion


I have not yet implemented Lifecycle Investing in any way, though I am intrigued by the concept, but I am still reviewing different ways to utilize leverage while minimizing risk.
