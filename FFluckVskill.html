Fantasy Football: Luck and Skill

Context: This work follows up on a previously written article about the relationship between luck and skill in fantasy football.

Objective: Use 2021 draft data to demonstrate that 'shared knowledge' has greatly increased the relative affect of luck in Fantasy Football.


Previous Research

In our league, there is no relationship between time dedication and sucess. This seems to directly contrast the majority opinion that Fantasy Football is a "game of skill"

I can show that time dedicated to our league showed no significant connection to historical win rates (Pearson Correlation of -0.19). The next step is to determine how shared knowledge plays a role in all of this.

Shared Knowledge

The chart above shows that preseason draft rankings are all very similar. However, they do a mediocre job of predicting actual performance. Moreso, the chart below shows that no one ranking seems better than any other.


This is what I am referring to as 'shared knowledge'. Different fantasy football sources all disseminate nearly identical information. So why do GMs stray from these sources and are they succesfful?


Research Hypothesis

Fantasy Football GMs that stray from rankings (demonstrating a belief in skill seperate from shared knowledge) will not perform better than those that adhere to rankings

The Relationship of Draft Adherence and Season Performance

Findings

From the chart above, one can see a positive correlation between a combined team FPPG and their similarity with Draft Rankings (greater than 0.7 in both cases). This data would reject my null and indeed support my belief that shared knowledge greatly reduces relative skill in Fantasy Football


Note: The data is similar when comparing only starters and when considering Total Fantasy Points instead of FFPG.

Findings

Null Hypothesis Rejected!

Research Limitations

Sample size was too small. These numbers should be compared with many more fantasy football leagues before any reasonable conclusions can be made.

Our draft was held a month before the rankings were posted. Data was manipulated as best as possible to acknowledge this, but the full effects are unknown.

Our league has slightly different rules and point calculations than the rankings assume. For more on this, see my work on Defensive Schemes
